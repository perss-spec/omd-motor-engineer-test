Senior Motor Design Engineer — Interview

Duration: 45-60 min 25 questions, 5 categories Expected: 5+ years BLDC/PMSM design

A Electromagnetic Fundamentals

1
Explain the difference between SPM (Surface Permanent Magnet) and IPM (Interior PM) topologies. When would you choose one over the other for a UAV propulsion motor?
Look for: reluctance torque contribution in IPM, saliency ratio Ld/Lq, field weakening capability, manufacturing complexity. SPM preferred for outrunner drones (simpler, higher torque density at low speed).
2
Derive the relationship between KV (RPM/V) and Kt (Nm/A). Why is Ke = Kt in SI units for a sinusoidal PMSM?
Kt = 60/(2π·KV) ≈ 9.55/KV for phase, or ≈ 8.27/KV line-to-line. Energy conservation: P = T·ω = E·I, therefore Kt = Ke.
3
For a 36-slot / 42-pole fractional-slot concentrated winding motor: calculate the winding factor. Why is this configuration popular for drone outrunner motors?
Kw = 0.933 (q = 2/7). High LCM = 252 reduces cogging. Symmetry = 6 reduces radial forces. Short end-windings = less copper loss, lighter.
4
How does cogging torque depend on the LCM of slots and poles? List 3 techniques to minimize it without changing the slot-pole combination.
Higher LCM = lower amplitude. Techniques: magnet arc shaping (bread-loaf), skewing (1 slot pitch), slot opening optimization, dummy slots, chamfered magnet edges.
5
Explain the field weakening region. What determines maximum speed? What is the "characteristic current" and why is it important?
I_char = λ_m / Ld. When I_char = I_rated → infinite CPSR (ideal FW). SPM has limited FW range due to low Ld. Maximum speed limited by DC bus voltage vs back-EMF.

B Materials & Component Selection

6
Compare N42SH, N45UH, and N52 magnet grades. Which would you select for an agricultural drone motor at ambient 40°C and winding temperature 130°C? Justify.
N42SH: Br = 1.30T, Tmax = 150°C, good margin. N52: Br = 1.45T but Tmax = 80°C — will demagnetize. N45UH: Tmax = 180°C, premium cost. N42SH is the sweet spot.
7
A motor with 21 pole pairs at 4000 RPM has electrical frequency ~1400 Hz. How does this affect lamination material and thickness selection?
At f > 400 Hz, eddy current losses dominate. P_eddy ~ f²·t². Need 0.2-0.35 mm laminations. M19 (0.35 mm) minimum. B20AV1200 (0.2 mm) or NO20 preferred for premium.
8
When is concentrated winding preferred over distributed? What are the trade-offs in harmonics, cogging, and copper losses?
Concentrated: short end-windings (less Cu loss, lighter), easier manufacturing. Downsides: higher space harmonics → more eddy losses in magnets. Distributed: smoother EMF, lower harmonics, but longer end-windings.
9
What factors determine the optimal air gap length in an outrunner motor? What happens if it's too small or too large?
Factors: mechanical clearance, magnet retention band, manufacturing tolerances. Too small: assembly risk, higher cogging. Too large: lower B_gap, reduced torque density, increased leakage flux.
10
Describe the demagnetization risk assessment process. How do you ensure the operating point stays above the BH curve "knee" at max current and temperature?
FEA at worst case: max d-axis current + max temperature. Check H_load vs Hcj with safety factor (H_load < 0.7·Hcj). Account for temperature coefficients of Br (−0.12%/°C) and Hcj (−0.6%/°C).

C Design Process & Methodology

11
Design a 3 kW outrunner motor for a heavy-lift multicopter. Walk through your process from requirements to first prototype.
Steps: sizing (D²L from TRV), slot/pole selection, magnetic circuit, winding design, FEA verification, thermal check, mechanical design (bell, shaft, bearings), prototype fabrication, dyno testing.
12
How do you model iron losses? Explain the difference between Steinmetz and Bertotti (three-term) models.
Steinmetz: P = k·f^a·B^b (empirical, single frequency). Bertotti: P = k_h·f·B² + k_e·f²·B² + k_a·f^1.5·B^1.5 (hysteresis + eddy + anomalous). Bertotti better for wide frequency range.
13
Explain aspect ratio (L/D) trade-offs. When prefer pancake vs. elongated geometry?
Pancake: higher torque (T ~ D²·L), lower inertia, fits UAV arms. Elongated: better cooling ratio, lower end-winding proportion. Drone motors: typically L/D = 0.2-0.5.
14
How would you set up multi-objective optimization for an outrunner motor? Variables, objectives, constraints, algorithm?
Variables: tooth width, slot opening, magnet thickness/arc, turns. Objectives: efficiency, weight, cogging. Constraints: Tmax, demagnetization, fill factor. Algorithms: NSGA-II, Bayesian, DOE + surrogate model.
15
Your 2D FEA predicts back-EMF but the prototype measures 12% lower. What are the possible causes?
Causes: magnet Br tolerance (−5%), temperature derating, 3D end-effects (2D overestimates by 5-10%), stacking factor, air gap larger than nominal, magnet assembly errors, wrong BH curve data.

D Thermal & Mechanical

16
What are the dominant cooling mechanisms in a drone outrunner motor at hover? LPTN or CFD — when to use which?
Prop-wash forced convection on rotating bell (h = 20-50 W/m²K). Conduction via stator teeth to housing. LPTN for early design (fast), CFD for detailed analysis. Radiation negligible.
17
Winding reaches 155°C instead of 130°C target at continuous operation. OD is fixed. What 3 changes would you prioritize?
1) Increase fill factor (VPI potting). 2) Better lamination steel (lower iron losses). 3) Optimize winding (fewer turns + higher voltage = lower I²R). 4) Ventilation holes in bell. 5) Upgrade slot liner thermal conductivity.
18
Bearing selection criteria for an outrunner drone motor. How to calculate L10h?
L10h = (C/P)^3 · 10&sup6;/(60·n). Factors: radial load, axial load, RPM, preload, sealing (2RS for IP55), ABEC class. Typical drone target: >1000 h at hover.
19
Magnet retention in an outrunner motor: compare bandaging (Kevlar/CF) vs adhesive-only at high RPM.
Centrifugal force F = m·ω²·r. Adhesive alone fails above certain RPM/temperature. Kevlar bandage adds safety margin. Carbon fiber: stronger but causes eddy current losses in magnets. Retention sleeve: safest but increases effective air gap.
20
How to achieve IP55 in an outrunner BLDC without excessive weight or thermal penalty?
Labyrinth seal at shaft/bell interface, epoxy potting of windings, sealed bearings (2RS), bell skirt overlap, vent holes with baffles. Trade-off: sealed = hotter, heavier.

E Practical Experience

21
Describe a motor project with significant trade-offs. What were the competing requirements and how did you decide?
Open-ended. Look for: structured decision process, quantitative trade-off analysis, stakeholder alignment, prototype validation.
22
How do you validate FEA against a prototype? What tests, what tolerances are acceptable?
Back-EMF vs RPM (<5%), locked-rotor torque, R/L (LCR meter), thermal run (thermocouples), cogging torque (torque sensor at low RPM), efficiency on dyno. Acceptable: <5% torque, <3% EMF.
23
Excessive vibration reported in a batch of 50 motors that passed bench tests. Diagnostic approach?
Rotor imbalance (most common), bearing defect, eccentricity, magnet delamination, resonance at operating RPM. Diagnostic: FFT vibration analysis, compare spectrum to healthy unit, check mechanical runout.
24
Customer wants cogging torque reduced 3x without changing outer envelope or magnet grade. Options?
Magnet arc shaping (~−50% cogging, ~−1% torque), stator skew 1 slot pitch (~−90% cogging, −3% Kw), slot opening optimization (~−30%, no torque loss). Combination can achieve >3x reduction.
25
What FEA/design tools have you used? Compare two you're most experienced with.
MotorCAD: fast multiphysics, template-based, design space exploration. Maxwell: detailed 2D/3D FEA, flexible geometry. JMAG: full optimization loop. FEMM: free, good for quick validation. Look for depth, not just tool names.

Design Assignment — High-Torque Outrunner Motor

Take-home assignment Estimated: 6-8 hours Deadline: 5 business days

Overview

OMD Systems is developing a propulsion system for a heavy-lift multirotor UAV platform. You are tasked with the electromagnetic design of the propulsion motor based on the requirements below. The goal is to maximize efficiency and thrust density while meeting all constraints.

You may use any EM design tool: MotorCAD, ANSYS Maxwell, JMAG, FEMM, MotorAnalysis-PM, or analytical calculations + Python. FEMM is recommended if you don't have commercial licenses.

2 Design Tasks

Task 2.1 — Topology Selection & Justification

Select and justify with quantitative reasoning:

  • Motor topology: outrunner SPM, outrunner IPM, inrunner, or axial flux
  • Slot/pole combination (with winding factor calculation)
  • Winding configuration: concentrated vs distributed, star vs delta
  • Target KV value (derive from thrust/propeller/voltage requirements)

Deliverable: 1-2 page justification with comparison table of ≥2 candidate configurations.

Task 2.2 — Electromagnetic Design

For the selected configuration, calculate or determine through FEA:

  • Magnetic circuit: B_gap, B_tooth, B_yoke flux densities
  • Winding: Turns/coil, wire gauge, parallel strands, fill factor, R_phase, L_d, L_q
  • Performance: Back-EMF waveform (shape + amplitude vs RPM), torque-current curve, cogging torque
  • Losses: Copper losses (hover + max thrust), iron losses (hysteresis + eddy), magnet eddy losses
  • Efficiency: At 25%, 50%, 75%, 100% throttle

Deliverable: FEA screenshots (flux density, back-EMF, cogging plots), calculation files, parameter summary table.

Task 2.3 — Material Selection

ComponentSpecify
Stator laminationGrade, thickness, core loss at operating frequency
Permanent magnetsGrade, Br, Hcj, BHmax, max temperature, dimensions
Magnet wireMaterial, gauge, insulation class, parallel strands
Rotor bellMaterial, wall thickness, surface treatment

Include a demagnetization check at maximum current and maximum operating temperature.

Task 2.4 — Thermal Assessment

Provide a thermal estimate for continuous hover operation:

  • Identify all heat sources and magnitudes
  • Estimate winding hot-spot temperature (LPTN or equivalent)
  • Confirm magnet temperature within safe limits
  • Suggest cooling strategy (passive/active, ventilation, potting)

Deliverable: Thermal circuit diagram, temperature estimates, cooling recommendations.

3 Challenge Task

★ Multi-Objective Optimization (Bonus)

Define and execute a multi-objective optimization:

  1. Maximize efficiency at hover (50% throttle)
  2. Minimize cogging torque (% of rated)
  3. Minimize motor weight (active materials)

Design variables (minimum 4):

  • Tooth width, slot opening, magnet thickness, magnet arc ratio, turns/wire gauge

Output: Pareto front, sensitivity analysis, recommended design point with justification.

Tools: Python + PyFEMM, MATLAB + FEA, MotorCAD optimizer, JMAG, or manual DOE.
